No Bakes

Description

No bakes are a type of cookie which - you guessed it! - aren't baked. They're delicious balls of chocolate, peanut butter, and oats, held together by the science of candy-making. But don't let the idea of candy-making scare you away! These are so easy to whip up that you could cook a batch every day (and after tasting them, you just might want to!)

Ingredients

  • 1 stick (8 tbsp) of unsalted butter
  • 2 cups brown sugar
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 1/4 cup cocoa powder
  • 2/3 cup peanut butter
  • 3 cups old-fashioned rolled oats
  • 1 tbsp vanilla extract
  • pinch of salt

Directions

  1. Melt the butter in a saucepan on medium heat.
  2. Stir in the brown sugar, milk, and cocoa powder until the sugar is dissolved.
  3. Allow the mixture to heat to a rolling boil. Boil for 2 minutes.
  4. Remove mixture from heat and mix in peanut butter, vanilla extract, and salt until the peanut butter is dissolved.
  5. Mix in oats.
  6. Using a spoon, create small balls of the mixture and place on wax paper.
  7. Allow to set until hardened. Placing the cookies in the fridge can help them set more quickly.
  8. Enjoy!
